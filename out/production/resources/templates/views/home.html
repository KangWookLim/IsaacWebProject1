"The Binding of Isaac" is a popular indie video game developed by Edmund McMillen and Florian Himsl. The game was initially released in 2011 and has seen multiple expansions and updates since then. It falls under the roguelike genre and is known for its challenging gameplay, randomly generated levels, and distinctive art style.
The game's storyline draws inspiration from the biblical tale of the binding of Isaac, where Abraham is tested by God and asked to sacrifice his son Isaac. In the game, players control a character named Isaac, a child who is trying to escape his mother's basement after she receives a message from God instructing her to sacrifice him. The gameplay involves navigating through procedurally generated dungeon-like levels, battling enemies, and facing various bosses.

Note
Chargers are a more advanced form of Maggot, and they're encountered more often as well, present in The Caves, The Catacombs and The Depths. As suggested by their names, they can charge toward Isaac when lining up.
They possess a smiling face of sorts, and look quite harmless as they slowly crawl along without attempting to follow Isaac. If a Charger lines up with Isaac vertically or horizontally, they will immediately and quickly charge in a straight line towards him. Because they're always encountered in groups of at least four, avoiding them quickly becomes a problem.
Chargers do not seem to notice Isaac unless he is moving. If Isaac stands still and a Charger lines up with him, they will continue crawling without attacking him.

Note
Attack Flies are red-and-black flashing type of fly enemies that will follow Isaac relentlessly until killed. Like regular Black Flies and few other types, they are commonly found in every chapter.
Their only way of damaging Isaac is by contact, and they move rather slowly, making them easy to kill. However, they can be dangerous when found in groups, as they can swarm around Isaac quickly. Attack Flies turn into regular Black Flies if Isaac has Skatole. They take a small amount of time to convert into Black Flies, so it is possible for Isaac to be damaged if the player is not careful.
